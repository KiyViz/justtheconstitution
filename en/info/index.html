About this site

justtheconstitution.org presents the full text of the United States Constitution and its twenty-seven amendments alongside facsimiles of the original parchment. No ads. No commentary. No paywall. Just the founding document, readable and free.

The site is open to everyone—students, educators, researchers, or anyone who wants to read the document that defines American governance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is this site?

A free, ad-free reading experience for the US Constitution. The full text with the original parchment scans displayed side by side.

Where does the text come from?

Articles I–VII and signatures are transcribed from the National Archives, preserving original spelling and punctuation. Amendments I–XXVII come from the National Constitution Center. Both sources were compared; no discrepancies were found.

Is this an official government website?

No. This is an independent project. The text and images are sourced from official government archives but this site is not affiliated with or endorsed by any government agency.

Can I download the text?

Yes. A Markdown download is available at the bottom of the reading page. It contains the complete document including all amendments.

Why is the spelling unusual?

The original 1787 document uses 18th-century English spelling and capitalization. We preserve it exactly as inscribed by Jacob Shallus on the parchment.

How do I cite this?

Use the copy button on any paragraph to get a formatted citation in Bluebook, MLA, or Chicago style. The citation engine generates the reference automatically.

Privacy
